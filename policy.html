Policy

My Journey

My interest in policy developed naturally as I studied economics, realizing how interconnected it is with both community and economics. The true spark came during my economic theory course in my third year, which opened my eyes to how policy decisions translate into real-world effects. One of my most meaningful experiences was writing my first major research paper on Africa, where I examined policy decisions and their direct impact. It helped put a face and name to the issues I’d seen firsthand through my family’s experiences in Somalia, strengthening my commitment to understanding and improving the systems that shape people’s lives.

Next Stages & Plans

  • Revise and expand my existing policy research to sharpen both content and analysis.
  • Regularly research, write, and explore new topics within the field of policy.
  • Look for ways to connect policy analysis directly with both economics and community issues.
  • Seek opportunities to meet, collaborate, and learn from policy professionals, researchers, and students.
  • Pursue roles and projects that connect policy, economics, and community development into one cohesive, impactful effort.
